# Operator Interview Guide

Target duration: 45 minutes. Extend to 60 minutes for a live workflow observation.

## 1. Role and operating context — 5 minutes

1. What work do you personally do in the invoice process?
2. How many people participate in it?
3. Approximately how many documents are processed monthly and at peak?
4. What types of clients, entities or business units are involved?
5. Which countries, currencies, languages and document types are common?

## 2. Walk through the most recent real batch — 15 minutes

Avoid asking what “usually” happens first. Anchor on a recent batch.

1. Where did the documents arrive?
2. How were documents downloaded, renamed, split, grouped or assigned?
3. How did you decide whether each file was an invoice, receipt, credit note, duplicate or something else?
4. Which fields did you enter or verify?
5. Did you enter line items?
6. What tools or screens were opened?
7. Which data was copied, transformed or looked up?
8. Where did another person become involved?
9. How was the final data imported or posted?
10. What happened to the original and exported files afterward?

For every step, ask:

- How often?
- How long?
- What can go wrong?
- How is the mistake detected?
- Who fixes it?

## 3. Timed observation — 10–15 minutes

Ask the participant to process one representative document or explain a named recent batch.

Record timestamps by stage using the workflow taxonomy. Do not interrupt during active work; ask clarification immediately after the step.

Observe:

- scrolling and zooming;
- copy/paste;
- manual calculations;
- supplier or tax lookups;
- context switches;
- reviewer questions;
- keyboard versus mouse actions;
- waiting on software;
- import-template cleanup;
- duplicate checks.

## 4. Errors and risk — 8 minutes

1. Tell me about the last invoice mistake that reached review or posting preparation.
2. Which fields are most frequently corrected?
3. Which errors are annoying but recoverable?
4. Which errors are financially unacceptable?
5. Which errors are difficult to detect later?
6. What source evidence do you need before approving an amount, date, party or tax value?
7. When do you ask the supplier or client for clarification?
8. How are duplicates detected?

## 5. Output and adoption — 5 minutes

1. What exact columns or fields must exist in the final output?
2. Would a reviewed CSV/XLSX be useful without direct posting?
3. Who would approve use of a new tool?
4. What security, residency or retention conditions apply?
5. What measured outcome would make you continue after a pilot?
6. What would make you stop using it immediately?

## 6. Close — 2 minutes

1. May I send you the workflow summary for verification?
2. Would you consider a supervised pilot using permissioned documents?
3. Who else should I interview to understand a different part of the process?
